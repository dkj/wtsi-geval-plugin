About the gEVAL Evaluation Browser

The gEVAL browser was initially created to aid in the sequencing and finishing endeavour of the zebrafish genome project, a project that still remains at Sanger, with major reference assemblies such as Zv9 and GRCz10 built and relased by the GRIT team. The gEVAL browser was built with the goals/scope to:

  • Navigate frequent genome assemblies not accessible by any other public genome browsers.
  • Represent datasets in a way to aid in the user of quickly identifying and navigating problematic regions or regions of interest.
  • To also provide information to allow the user to resolve these regions so that they can be corrected in a subsequent future release of the assembly.

The gEVAL browser is managed by the Genome Reference Informatics Team (GRIT) at the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute.

The GRIT team is composed of computer biologists/curators and bioinformaticians, whose goal is to reveal and correct quality issues and to identify and add variation for various genome assemblies, many present in the gEVAL browser.

Some of the Projects the GRIT team is working on:

  • Improvement of the human reference assembly, correcting errors and adding variation
  • Correcting and improving the draft assemblies of 16 mouse strains
  • Adding missing gene loci to the mouse reference assembly
  • Creating new zebrafish assemblies from different strains, developing techniques to enable haplotype resolution
  • Improving the zebrafish reference assembly through addition of more clone sequence and integration with a new de novo assembly

For more information regarding the Team's work at the Sanger Insitute and for individual member's profile please visit the group's Sanger site.
