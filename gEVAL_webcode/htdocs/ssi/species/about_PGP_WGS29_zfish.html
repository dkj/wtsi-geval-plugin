The zebrafish genome project is a collaboration between the Sanger Institute and the zebrafish community, announced during the Sanger Institute Zebrafish Workshop 2000 and was started in February 2001.


The zebrafish WGS29 gEVAL viewer represents the whole genome sequence assembly generated from a single Tuebingen, double haploid zebrafish. The assembly is based on 13,756,367 reads comprising 10,891,216,277 bp with a coverage of 5.5x. Phusion and phrap was used to build the consenus contigs, with read pair information used to build supercontigs. Small supercontigs with less than 3 reads or smaller than 0.5 kb were rejected. WGS29 was used in the building of the zebrafish genome assembly Zv7, by integrating wgs sequence into gap regions in the clone assembly.
