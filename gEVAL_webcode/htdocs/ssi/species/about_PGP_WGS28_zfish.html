The zebrafish genome project is a collaboration between the Sanger Institute and the zebrafish community, announced during the Sanger Institute Zebrafish Workshop 2000 and was started in February 2001.


The zebrafish WGS28 gEVAL viewer represents the whole genome sequence assembly generated from a single Tuebingen, double haploid zebrafish. The WGS assembly is based on 20,541,433 reads comprising 14,160,626,498 bp with a coverage of 6.5-7x. WGS28 was used in the building of the zebrafish genome assembly Zv8, by integrating wgs sequence into gap regions in the clone assembly.
